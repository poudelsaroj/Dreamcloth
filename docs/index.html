Abstract

We propose a general research framework for distilling physically plausible simulation parameters from minimal visual input by leveraging the "knowledge of the world" embedded in pretrained video generation models. Given a single monocular image, we reconstruct a 3D scene hypothesis (body + cloth), run a differentiable physics simulation (MPM-based cloth dynamics with contact), and render a short video. We then compute score-distillation gradients from a frozen video diffusion model to iteratively refine physical parameters until the rendered dynamics align with real-world motion statistics.

This reframes inverse simulation as a form of foundation-model supervision, extending recent advances that distill dynamics priors from video generators or combine video diffusion with MPM for material learning. For clothed humans, we also build on the concept of physics-aware avatar reconstruction and motion-aware dynamic clothed avatars, but shift the emphasis toward monocular scalability and parameter identifiability.

From this pipeline, the physical parameters of clothes (e.g., stiffness, damping, friction, density) can be estimated from single images, enabling calibrated garments that can be resized and resimulated across bodies. This directly targets the long-standing sizing gap in virtual try-on (VTON), by recovering garment-specific physics and geometry that generalize across sizes rather than relying on fixed-category priors.

The broader impact is a reusable recipe for parameter estimation without expensive capture: as physics-grounded video models improve, the score signal becomes more informative, enabling more accurate material recovery and richer domains (fluids, fracture, soft tissue). The project's key deliverable is a principled pipeline‚Äîsimulation, rendering, and distillation‚Äîthat produces interpretable, reusable physics parameters at scale.

Key Insight

Pretrained video diffusion models encode rich priors about how materials move in the real world. By computing score-distillation gradients between rendered physics simulations and these "world priors," we can recover accurate physical parameters without expensive lab measurements or multi-view capture setups.

Method Overview

Overview of DreamCloth. Given a single monocular image, we (1) reconstruct a 3D scene hypothesis with body and cloth geometry, (2) run differentiable MPM-based physics simulation with learnable parameters, (3) render the simulated motion, and (4) compute score-distillation loss against a frozen video diffusion model to optimize physical parameters.

Pipeline

1

Scene Reconstruction

Reconstruct 3D body and cloth geometry from a single monocular image using state-of-the-art reconstruction methods.

2

Differentiable Physics

Simulate cloth dynamics using Material Point Method (MPM) with learnable physical parameters and contact handling.

3

Neural Rendering

Render the simulated motion sequence into a video for comparison with video diffusion priors.

4

Score Distillation

Compute gradients from frozen video diffusion model to iteratively refine physics parameters.
